Youth Ministry.

To develop an effective, dynamic, and growing army of youth effectually living the Christian life as they walk with their Lord Jesus Christ.

Primary Objective Of Youth Department


• To instruct and train the youth to understand and implement the Word of God in their lives, especially as it relates to: Worship, Service, Outreach, Christian Growth, and Family.
• To actively integrate the youth into all activities of church life here at JCC-Agape. Envisioning and equipping them to lead and to serve here, now, and in the future.
• To seek out lay leadership and effectively train them to work with the youth and others as part of a ministry team.
• To help keep our church’s intentional-intergenerational vision foremost in everyone’s heart and ministry.
• To develop an older-youth component. This would be an outreach, traveling, ministry team made up of mostly older, well-disciplined committed youth.
• To work closely with all other members of the church Leaders, effectively uniting with and complementing other church ministries.
• To assist families, especially families with teenagers, to assimilate into church ministry as a family, learning to minister together as a family.
• To guide the church youth in cultivating a burden for -Christians and designing and implementing effective outreach and assimilation strategies.

This department provides opportunity for youth:


• To get to know and establish a positive relationship with each young person in the congregation.
• To provide a regularly youth service that is worshipful, fun, relevant, and effective in instructing, training, nurturing, and launching youth into Christian service.
• To attend leadership meetings endeavoring to work closely with other leaders to coordinate the youth activities, calendar and vision with all other areas of church life.
• To undergo mentoring and coaching.
• To make sure that the females in the youth group have effective, caring role-models and easy access to female counselors when appropriate.
• To coordinate youth-oriented retreats, outings and camp activities.
• To share their gifts and talents in all areas of the church ministry as time permits.
• To set a good example in the areas of relationships, commitment, follow-through and responsible living.
• In a very permissive era, help youth to establish Biblical guidelines for appropriate behavior, especially as it regards the opposite sex.
• To model high standards in their own personal, family and devotional life. Maintaining a good example in their relationships with others, studying, praying, tithing, and such. • To pray for their parents, guardians and involve their parents in their activities
